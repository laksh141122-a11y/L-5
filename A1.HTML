Sandwich day

The sandwich is believed to have been invented in the 18th century by John Montagu, who was known for his love of gambling. To avoid interrupting his card games, he requested that his meat be served between two slices of bread, allowing him to eat without using utensils. This practical solution quickly gained popularity, leading others to order their meals "the same as Sandwich," which eventually led to the term "sandwich" being coined


Sandwich Day celebrates this convenient and versatile food item, which has evolved into countless variations worldwide. From simple peanut butter and jelly sandwiches to elaborate club sandwiches, the possibilities are endless. The day serves as a reminder of the sandwich's cultural significance and its role as a beloved meal option across different cuisines.
